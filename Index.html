    "Let food be thy medicine and medicine be thy food"

    Book a Session

    You grew up with it, you live because of it and you definitely cannot do without it! Sometimes you have it even when you do not want it; sometimes you are bored, happy, sad, somewhere in between with mixed feelings, but the very thought of it makes you glad. You share a love hate relationship with it when the scale flips. Sometimes sweet, sour, tangy, spicy but always tasty.

    ​

    In all its forms, it has never failed to save a date, celebrate an occasion and even mourn the grief of a loved one. Vibrant with colours of traditions, rich as our cultures, diverse as each of us, flavours that handed down from one generation to the next with delicate care and love. The fruit of the soil, on the altar, it is blessed and on the table, shared. It brings people together, gives form to love and warms the hearts of those who receive it with gratitude.

    Magic is truly in one’s kitchen. The ability to create, recreate and even destroy, the power to heal and injure, the blessing to nurture with every ingredient you choose is mesmerizing. The rendezvous is just something out of the world. The way we play with physics and chemistry at home to perform reactions that bring us to the desired conclusion is commendable.

    ​

    With great power comes great responsibility; so if you are a good cook, it is equally important to be able to preserve the nutrients of all your creations. To have the love for food and at the same time know to balance the act with just the right nutrition is like adding the correct amount of salt to your recipe.

    ​

    And last but not the least, every dish or recipe has a story, some of which are cherished very dearly. NutriTales is about each of those stories. One such tale is of a foodie and nutritionist who explores the limitless possibilities of love, good food and binds it together with threads of nutrition.
